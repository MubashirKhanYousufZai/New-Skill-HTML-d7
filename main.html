Talha Anjum

Introduction

Talha anjum (born 3 October 1996) is a Pakistani rapper, songwriter and lyricist. He was born and raised in Karachi and is popularly known for being a member and co-founder of the Hip hop music band Young Stunners along with Talhah Yunus, and Jokhay as a music producer

Early life and education

Talha Anjum was born on 3 October 1996 in Karachi, Pakistan.[5][6] He received his primary education at the Army Public School in Karachi. He has two siblings.[7][8][9]

Releases

He got his first outbreak after 5 years by the song"Burger-e-Karachi", which was released in 2013. Some of his major hits include "Tum Tum", "Gumaan", "Don't Mind", "Afsanay", "Downers at Dusk", and "Groove Mera", the anthem of PSL 6.[3][4]
Carreir
Anjum did rap battles during his school time. Anjum used fundamental issues and references to gain audience applicability. Talhah Yunus and Anjum realized their potential and formed a duo band known as the 'Young Stunners' based in Karachi. Their music is produced by Jokhay. Their major hits are: the collaborative 2021 PSL anthem Groove Mera,[10] performance at the 2021 Pakistan Day Parade[11] and music video song named "Afsanay".[12][13][14] They helped introduce Urdu rap to the Pakistani music industry.[15][16][17][18]
